# Effectiveness Scorecard

## Current Score

| Dimension | Score | Readout |
| --- | ---: | --- |
| Strategic uniqueness | 8.5/10 | Strong wedge: property buyer protection, bank trust layer, compliance, and Web3 evidence in one concrete story. |
| Bank relevance | 8/10 | Clear SCB-like value: capture high-value settlement flow, create escrow/compliance revenue, reduce risk before money moves. |
| Demo clarity | 7/10 | The story is understandable, but the product needs a more guided real-user workflow. |
| Technical credibility | 7/10 | Backend tests, live API hash, frontend, and privacy-safe attestation are in place. RAG trace/export would raise credibility. |
| Wow factor | 6.5/10 | The idea is memorable, but the current demo can still feel like a pitch page without guided simulation. |
| Production path | 7/10 | Roadmap is credible if boundaries stay clear: not legal guarantee, not full KYC, not NFT title. |

## Main Finding

The project is strong enough to show as a serious hackathon MVP. The largest next gain is not another heavy integration. It is making the existing product feel like a real bank-assisted property transaction.

## What Should Improve First

1. Show the buyer under deposit pressure.
2. Show the suspicious payment instruction.
3. Explain the exact red flags.
4. Show the bank counter-offer.
5. Show compliance approval.
6. Show the Closing Passport hash and evidence preview.

## Go/No-Go Readiness

Ready for a confident 3-minute demo when the viewer can answer:

- why the buyer is in danger;
- why direct money movement should pause;
- why the bank is useful;
- why escrow is the recommended route;
- what the Closing Passport proves;
- why Web3 is used for auditability instead of property ownership.
