# Stakeholder Perspectives

Ordered by primary customer hierarchy. See `docs/MONEY_INFRASTRUCTURE_THESIS.md` for the full thesis.

## Bank Perspective

For banks, the product is a settlement trust rail and money OS for property — not a listing marketplace.

Potential bank value:

- capture high-value flow before it leaves bankable rails;
- onboard affluent foreign buyers;
- sell escrow, compliance, and FET-ready settlement packages;
- reduce reputational risk from suspicious transfers;
- stay involved after purchase through rental income accounts, insurance, maintenance payments, tax reminders, resale, and wealth management.

Risk:

Banks will not want to assume title or legal liability.

Bank verdict:

> Attractive if the bank verifies settlement readiness and controls funds release without guaranteeing property title.

## Compliance Perspective

Compliance teams need a structured review path before money moves in high-value property settlement.

Potential value:

- structured approve / conditional / reject / escalate workflow;
- evidence packs with deterministic rules plus AI-assisted retrieval;
- audit trail without PII on-chain;
- human-in-loop remains final decision authority.

Compliance verdict:

> Model output is advisory. Final decision remains human-in-the-loop and role-based.

## Government And Brand Alignment Perspective

The network can support a cleaner foreign property purchase market and align market quality with Kingdom positioning.

Potential public value:

- clearer foreign inflow traceability;
- better purpose-of-transfer evidence;
- fewer disputed deposits;
- less incentive for grey P2P routes;
- verification layer where commission models admit unverified intermediaries;
- trusted channel for high-quality market participants.

Boundary:

The network must not claim to replace regulators, lawyers, land offices, or official title verification.

Government verdict:

> Useful if positioned as risk-based evidence and settlement readiness, not private legal approval.

## Verified Developer And Agent Perspective

Good market participants gain differentiation where commission models currently admit anyone without skill verification.

Potential value:

- verified status on bankable settlement paths;
- cleaner payment instructions and escrow release;
- reduced friction with source-of-funds review;
- separation from hit-and-run intermediaries that damage market reputation.

Boundary:

Verified status is platform/network evidence, not official government licensing.

Participant verdict:

> Good developers and agents gain trust; banks route settlement only through verified paths.

## Business Perspective

Bankable Property Network is stronger than a single compliance app because it can become transaction infrastructure.

Potential revenue:

- escrow and compliance package fees;
- verified developer and agent status;
- bank referral or onboarding economics;
- post-purchase financial services.

Risk:

- network businesses are harder to launch than single-bank tools;
- the product needs one strong anchor use case before becoming a marketplace-scale network.

Business verdict:

> Start with the Closing Passport vertical slice, then expand into trusted settlement rails.

## Buyer Perspective — Social Bonus Layer

The buyer is not the primary customer. Buyer value is a **side effect** of money infrastructure working correctly.

Buyer value:

- avoid paying the wrong legal entity;
- understand which funds are green, amber, or red;
- get a bankable route instead of ad hoc agent instructions;
- receive a Closing Passport evidence pack for bank, lawyer, developer, or dispute follow-up.

Buyer verdict:

> Deposit protection matters — but it is the social bonus of bank-grade rails, not the reason the OS exists.
